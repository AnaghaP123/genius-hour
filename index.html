How does body language's meaning change depending on the place/person you are talking to?

Otherwise, here is the answer to the research question.

The meaning of body language can change depending on the environment, the situation, and the people. If the people were very cultural, their definitions of body language would be different than yours. For example, something as simple as a firm handshake can be seen as authoratative by Westerners and aggressive by Easterners. Additionally, the situation/context can change its meaning. In a police station, officers don't look at the major expressin of your face, they look at the microexpressions. The small, uncontrollable, burst of emotions. After being asked a certain question (typically pertaining to the crime), these microexpressions help steer the line of questioning.


In conclusion, body language is an important part of everday life, which has a meaning that can be changed depending on certain aspects (context, people).
